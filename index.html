FAQ

Common questions from moms

Short, clear answers you can trust.

Do you accept insurance?

We provide education-based wellness services that are not billed to insurance. We’re unable to submit claims and reimbursement isn’t available. Many families use an HSA/FSA card for sessions. To keep costs sensible, we keep labs optional and recommend only a few targeted supports at a time.

Do you offer virtual sessions?

Yes. Select services are available virtually via Zoom or phone for education and coaching. Hands-on services (like muscle testing) are offered in person.

Who do you work with?

Mostly moms and families looking for gentle, realistic steps. Common goals include energy, digestion, skin, sleep, focus, and stress resilience. Our lens is simple: reduce burdens, support basics, and listen to what your body is showing—without making medical claims.

Do you prescribe medications?

No. We do not diagnose conditions or prescribe pharmaceuticals. Our recommendations tend to be nutrition, lifestyle, and select nutraceuticals or gentle practices. Please consult your licensed provider for medical concerns and prescriptions.

Are your plans “one size fits all”?

No. Each plan is tailored. We move in phases, address priorities in the order your body shows, and keep action steps doable for busy family life.

What products do you use or recommend?

We keep supplements minimal and intentional, focusing on quality brands that fit your needs and budget. We do not require purchases and we’re happy to review what you already have (education-only).

Do you see children? What about pregnancy or breastfeeding?

Yes—many families bring their children. For pregnancy or breastfeeding, we focus on gentle lifestyle support and education; we avoid introducing new supplements unless your licensed provider approves. As always, please follow your provider’s guidance.

I’m very sensitive—can you still help?

We regularly work with highly sensitive clients. We start small, reduce stressors, and pace changes so your system stays as calm as possible. Your comfort sets the speed.

What does “open drainage & detox pathways” mean?

Before adding supports, we make sure basics are moving well—bowel regularity, gentle hydration, lymph movement, and everyday habits that help your body process life’s load. This often reduces “push too hard, feel worse” reactions.

Do you review lab work?

We don’t order or interpret labs medically. If you bring recent labs from your provider, we can review them for education and to help you ask good questions at your next appointment.

I’ve tried so many things—should I still have hope?

Yes. We take a gentle, step-by-step approach that reduces overwhelm and focuses on what your body is actually showing today. Our aim is clarity and confidence, not perfection. You are not alone.
